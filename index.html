EEG (Electroencephalogram) recordings are among the most sensitive and clinically vital signals in neuroscience — yet they are inherently fragile. Eye blinks, muscle contractions, and environmental electrical noise constantly corrupt the data, making accurate interpretation challenging.

This project deploys a deep learning Autoencoder trained to identify and suppress these artifacts. Given a noisy EEG input, the model encodes it into a compressed latent representation and reconstructs a cleaner approximation of the original neural signal.

The accompanying GUI allows non-technical users to load signals, trigger processing, and compare raw vs. cleaned outputs side-by-side — bridging the gap between research and clinical utility.

Key Features

  • EEG signal upload & multi-channel visualization
  • Automated normalization & preprocessing
  • Artifact simulation (EOG, EMG, electrical noise)
  • Autoencoder-based signal denoising
  • Clean signal reconstruction & export
  • Side-by-side raw vs. processed comparison

02 // Methodology

Processing Pipeline

01
EEG Signal Input
Raw EEG recordings loaded via the GUI from CSV files. The dataset contains 14 channels captured at standard clinical frequencies, including eye-state labels for validation.
02
Preprocessing & Normalization
Signals are normalized to a standardized range using min-max scaling. This ensures consistent input magnitude across all 14 channels regardless of recording conditions or electrode impedance variation.
03
Artifact Simulation
Realistic noise is synthetically injected to simulate eye-blink (EOG) artifacts, muscle activity (EMG), and environmental 50/60Hz electrical interference — creating corrupted training pairs.
04
Autoencoder Training
A deep convolutional Autoencoder is trained on noisy→clean signal pairs. The encoder compresses the signal into a low-dimensional latent space, forcing the network to learn only the essential neural structure.
05
Signal Reconstruction
The decoder maps the latent representation back to a full-resolution denoised EEG signal. Reconstruction error (MSE ≈ 0.01) quantifies the fidelity of the output vs. the ground truth clean signal.
06
GUI Visualization & Comparison
The Tkinter-based GUI renders both the noisy input and the reconstructed signal on Matplotlib canvases in real time, enabling visual and quantitative comparison in under 2 seconds.
